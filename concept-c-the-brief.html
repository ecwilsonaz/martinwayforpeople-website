City approves 1,693 daily car trips on deadliest pedestrian corridor — with no safety analysis

A 90-page traffic study for the proposed Chick-fil-A at 4505 Martin Way E contains no pedestrian safety analysis. The appeal has been filed. Public comment will determine what happens next.

I. The Record
What the traffic study omits

The Traffic Impact Analysis prepared for the applicant analyzes vehicle delay at five intersections. It does not contain any pedestrian volume counts, any pedestrian level-of-service analysis, or any assessment of how 1,693 new daily vehicle trips affect pedestrian safety on Martin Way.

The TIA's own crash data documents pedestrian crashes — then never analyzes them. The TRPC's Martin Way Crossing Strategy, completed during the project review, identifies the project site as a priority crossing location. The traffic study does not reference this finding.

II. Key Findings
Five facts the hearing examiner should know
  1. The traffic study contains zero pedestrian safety analysis despite documenting pedestrian crashes in its own data
  2. Martin Way has the highest pedestrian fatality rate of any corridor in Thurston County
  3. TRPC identified the project site as one of 10 priority crossing locations — the study ignores this
  4. 357 daily bus riders at adjacent stops cross a 4-lane road with no marked crosswalks
  5. The city is spending $1.637M on pedestrian safety on this corridor while approving a project that adds 1,693 daily trips without studying the impact
