Martin Way for People
Appeal filed · Permits frozen

Zero pedestrian safety analysis on the deadliest corridor in the county

The City of Olympia approved a Chick-fil-A dual drive-through — 1,693 new daily car trips — on Martin Way, where 27 pedestrians have been struck and 2 killed. The 90-page traffic study never analyzes pedestrian safety. We filed an appeal. Permits are frozen. A public hearing is coming.

The traffic study ignores its own data

The TIA's crash table lists pedestrian crashes — then never analyzes them. 90 pages of vehicle delay metrics. Zero pages of pedestrian safety analysis.

TRPC flagged this exact location

The Martin Way Crossing Strategy identified 4500 Martin Way as one of 10 priority crossing locations. The traffic study doesn't reference this finding.

357 daily bus riders, no crosswalks

Riders at the two adjacent bus stops must cross a four-lane undivided arterial with no marked crosswalk to reach the other side of Martin Way.

The city knows this road is dangerous

Olympia has programmed $1.637M for pedestrian safety improvements on Martin Way — then approved a project adding 1,693 daily trips without studying pedestrian impact.

Writing your comment

The hearing examiner rules on whether the environmental review was adequate. Here's what carries weight.

Focus on these
  • The traffic study contains no pedestrian safety analysis
  • Martin Way has the highest pedestrian fatality rate in the county
  • 1,693 new daily trips on a corridor with no crosswalks near the site
  • The city is spending $1.6M on pedestrian safety on this same road
  • TRPC identified this location as a priority crossing — the study ignores it
  • Your personal experience walking, biking, or riding transit on Martin Way
Less effective for this hearing
  • Corporate values or donation history
  • General opposition to chain restaurants
  • Economic impact arguments
  • Complaints about the brand or franchise model
