What happened

A 90-page traffic study that never mentions the people on foot

In February 2026, the City of Olympia issued a Determination of Nonsignificance for a new Chick-fil-A at 4505 Martin Way E — the former Red Lobster site. The project will generate 1,693 net new vehicle trips per day on a four-lane undivided arterial with no marked pedestrian crossings near the site.

The Traffic Impact Analysis, prepared by a consultant hired by Chick-fil-A, runs approximately 90 pages. It analyzes vehicle delay at five intersections. It contains no pedestrian volume counts, no pedestrian level-of-service analysis, and no assessment of how nearly 1,700 new daily car trips will affect the people who walk along and cross Martin Way.

The Thurston Regional Planning Council identified the project site — 4500 Martin Way — as one of only 10 priority locations for new pedestrian crossings along the entire 7-mile corridor. The traffic study doesn't reference this finding.

Two bus stops adjacent to the project site serve approximately 357 daily riders — people on foot, walking to and from stops on a road with no marked crosswalks. The traffic study contains no analysis of these pedestrian movements.
